Ordinance concerning the Extension of the Widows’ and Orphans’ Fund to Life Annuities.

Eutin, 11 March 1782.


[Printed on a quarto leaf. Cf. above Correspondence LI, p. 183.]

"At a time, when the wavering, and threatened with collapse, Bremen and Calenberg Widow Funds give so much occasion to hear and to read about them: the few well-founded institutions of this kind — the new Danish Widow Fund, the Mecklenburg-Schwerin, and the Oldenburg Widow Funds, as well as the Hamburg General Institution of Provision — might well deserve to become more widely known; especially the latter, the Hamburg G. I. Institution, on account of its wide scope, and because it stands open to every one even outside Hamburg, whereas the other mentioned institutions are restricted to the inhabitants of their respective lands."

Enclosed is the Ordinance concerning the Extension of the present Institution. – I must further add, regarding our Life-Annuity Fund, that since many people are accustomed to ask in percentages, I have appended the calculation in percentages: namely, how with 14 Rthlr. 39 Gr., 1 Rthlr. is purchased; thus with 100 Rthlr. contribution, 6 Rthlr. and 88/100 of a Thaler life annuity are purchased, and so forth. One of the two, either the contribution or the life annuity, must be expressed in a round number: and since the Fund cannot engage in fractions at payments which fall every half year, the life annuity must necessarily be determined in whole Thalers: whereas in the contribution, a payment which occurs only once and is entered in the books, a fraction is of no consequence.

Permit me further, on this occasion, to bring into view the French life annuities, before and under Necker. Only between the 57th and 58th year does the pension rate of 10 per cent enter with us Oldenburgers: how then can the King of France, without his manifest loss, or without the intention of one day not keeping his word, promise 10 per cent upon every single head, without distinction of age, and even 9 per cent upon two heads? Let it be that the French financier perhaps assumes the interest rate of 5 per cent, whereas we in Oldenburg calculate with 4 per cent: still it will not do; and it is strange, that a government which has an Académie des Sciences at hand, does not suffer itself to be told by the same what is feasible or not."

Oldenburg, 12 Oct. 1782.
Oeder.
By the Grace of God We, Friedrich August, Bishop of Lübeck, Heir to Norway, Duke of Schleswig, Holstein, Stormarn, and of the Dithmarschen, reigning Duke of Oldenburg, etc. etc.
Do hereby make known, that the paternal care for necessitous persons among Our subjects, which moved Us, by Our most gracious Ordinance of the 1st of November 1779, to the establishment of a Widow and Orphan Fund, doth now further serve Us as motive, to take into consideration yet another class of such needy persons, whose subsistence resteth upon a small capital, which, owing to the uncertain duration of life, they dare not encroach upon, and who yet cannot live upon the mere interest thereof. For the benefit and better sustenance of such persons, We have judged it good, hereby and by this means, likewise to establish a Life-Annuity Fund, and thus to extend the aforesaid Institution for the Widows and Orphans thereto, and in such wise.

I. Since this Institution is intended solely for the truly needy, and indeed only for such as are Our subjects: the following are to be reckoned therein—

a. all and every person who, either in mind or in body, is not in a condition to gain his subsistence, and whose estate doth not suffice thereto; such as the insane, the feeble-minded, the sickly and infirm; also aged persons, who can no longer labour, and who have no children obliged and able to provide for them.

b. Those, who indeed are themselves healthy, and by labour can with difficulty earn their bread: herein are chiefly to be reckoned the unprovided daughters of servants and of persons of rank and repute, and their childless widows; namely, present widows, with the entire exclusion of all future widows of such men as were not properly bound to enter into the Widow Fund, but who nevertheless might place contributions therein.

II. To the Direction of the Widow and Orphan Fund, under whose supervision this Life-Annuity Fund shall be placed, is reserved the cognizance and decision concerning the admission or non-admission of members; with permission to lay before Us whatever special circumstances may occur: and no one, to whom the Direction should refuse admission, shall have any right to complain thereof.

III. All that the Ordinance concerning the Widow and Orphan Fund prescribes, permits, and promises in §§ 1, 2, 3, 7, 9–12, with regard to the standard of coinage, the due terms, the time of registration, the exemption from the use of stamped paper, and the exemption of pensions from all claims, doth likewise, in due measure, apply with respect to these life annuities; and it is here only to be noted, concerning the registration and the cited § 3, that nothing further is required than a valid receipt for the sum received.

IV. The life annuity shall not be fixed in portions of 10 Rthlr., as are the widow and orphan pensions, but by single Reichsthalers; and it may be taken from 1 Rthlr. up to 500 Rthlr. yearly, as the highest life annuity. The prices for each single Thaler are to be seen from the following table:Alter: Jare. Preis: Reichstaler und Groschen:

Wherein it is to be noted, that the table therefore beginneth only with the 40th year of life, and endeth with the 80th year, because the admission of younger and older persons is not easily to be presumed; yet such younger and elder persons are not on that account to be deemed excluded, but, should the case occur, the prices also for every lesser and greater age of those applying shall be made known by the Direction.

V. The Life-Annuity Fund shall remain a fund by itself, and a separate book shall be kept therefor; just as the Widow Fund, the Orphan Fund, and the Subsidiary Fund remain separated in calculation, though in the administration and investment of the monies they constitute one whole.

VI. Moreover, Our most graciously promised sovereign guarantee to the Widow and Orphan Fund extendeth likewise to this Life-Annuity Fund; so that unto each one the purchased life annuity shall be rendered, without diminution, until the day of his death.

As that which all present and future participants shall deem binding.

Documented under Our own hand and signature, and the affixed Ducal seal. Given at Our Prince-Bishopric Residence at the Castle of Eutin, the 11th of March 1782.

(L. S.) Fried. August.
