Another Observation for the Examination of Widow’s Funds.

On the occasion of the recently established widow and orphan fund for the Prince-Bishopric of Lübeck and this Duchy, various inquiries have been made in these lands regarding married couples from past times. I herewith share, for the common benefit, the report received from Delmenhorst, compiled with particular care, concerning 154 couples who were married there during the 11 years from 1730 to 1740.

This period of time, and no more remote one, was chosen because otherwise the inquiry would have become too burdensome and incomplete; and no more recent one, because there would otherwise be too many persons still living, whose future life span would have to be determined conjecturally, so that the experience with such couples would not constitute sufficient factual evidence.

In total, 235 couples were in fact married in Delmenhorst during the mentioned period; but among them were 58 men, mostly military personnel, about whom and their wives the necessary information could not be obtained at all. For another 23 couples, the information was incomplete. The remaining 154 couples, for whom complete information could be collected, are listed with the preserved names from the register provided by the reverend clergy of Delmenhorst.

Those who might be inclined to raise the minor objection against the observation presented in this Museo in April 1779 — which concerned 200 Hamburg preachers — that these were not married couples but persons of the same sex, now have here an experience drawn from actual married couples.

How such experiences are to be used and processed, I have shown in the aforementioned essay; I therefore add nothing further here, except an exhortation to all overseers of widow’s funds to apply this observation to their institutions. The conclusion — that just as the result would have been, had these couples from former times (to whom the Süßmilchian decrement table otherwise applies) been members of the institution now under review, so likewise will the result be for those presently living or to be admitted in the future — this conclusion has undeniable validity, and it is quite in vain to attempt to evade the consequences of such an application.

In the following register, Column I shows the year of marriage; Column II the number from the Delmenhorst register; Column III the time of death — of the men in the first row, of the women in the second row; Column IV the attained age of the men and women, the former in the first, the latter in the second row.

Oldenburg, 22 January 80.
G. C. Oeder.
